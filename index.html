How to Train

Mode 1 — Triadic Entailment

Every trial contains exactly three relational statements using three letters. The first two statements establish the transformed relation among the letters. The third statement is the relation being tested.

Example: “A is west of B; B is north of C; C is southeast of A.” The first two relations place A northwest of C, so the tested inverse relation is accurate and the triad is a MATCH.

The letters are arbitrary identifiers. MATCH never depends on identical lettering across trials. It depends only on whether the third relation is logically accurate after composing the first two transformations.

The triad itself contains no “therefore,” answer explanation or rule label. MATCH or NO MATCH is explained separately only after the player responds.

Meta-distinction

Difficulty progresses from cardinal to eight-way and sixteen-way relations. The player must distinguish exact entailment from adjacent direction, reversed subject–object orientation, a relation attached to the wrong letters, branch-versus-chain structure, cancellation and local consistency that fails globally.

Consistent renaming, inverse wording and harmless premise reordering preserve the logic. A small change to one binding or direction may change the answer completely.

Mode 2 — Ontological Integration

The previous ontology-category system is preserved here. Each sentence contains three independently transformed symbols. Their ontology categories and forms create three node transformations; two cardinal transitions bind the roles; and the transition between those transitions creates a higher-order turn.

The nine categories are All, Difference, Action, Division, Connection, Multiplication, Projection, Encompassment and Completion. N-back scoring compares the derived integrated ontology and integrated form, while complete triadic logic drives cognitive interference. Letters remain surface carriers in Mode 2.

Accessibility guarantee

The program constructs one final three-statement string, writes that exact string to the live visual region and sends the same string to speech synthesis. The response window opens only after speech finishes; audio-only display, pause/resume replay, speech rate, volume and speech/background separation remain available.

This is a theoretically motivated relational-integration design. It is not validated evidence that training will increase general fluid intelligence.
